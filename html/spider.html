🏠 Common Household Spiders

These are the spiders most likely sharing your home right now. Most are completely harmless and actually help by eating pest insects.

🕷️

Common House Spider

Parasteatoda tepidariorum

Builds messy cobwebs in corners, windows, and behind furniture. Found worldwide. Non-aggressive and completely harmless.

Corners & ceilings Worldwide ○ Harmless
🕷️

Cellar Spider (Daddy Long-Legs)

Pholcus phalangioides

Extremely long, thin legs and a tiny body. Vibrates rapidly in its web when disturbed. Feeds on other spiders, including redbacks.

Basements & corners Worldwide ○ Harmless
🕷️

Yellow Sac Spider

Cheiracanthium spp.

Pale yellow or beige; builds a small silk tube retreat in corners and folds of fabric. Most common spider to bite humans indoors.

Walls & fabrics North America, Europe ● Low Risk
🕷️

Garden Spider

Argiope aurantia

Large, striking black and yellow patterned spider. Builds perfect orb webs with a distinctive zigzag silk band through the centre.

Gardens & hedges North America, Europe ○ Harmless
